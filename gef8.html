Project Context

A Landscape Under Threat

The Gulf and Western Provinces hold some of the world's last great tropical forests, with 74% of Gulf and 82% of Western Province classified as primary forest. These landscapes include the vital Fly, Purari, and Kikori river systems.

However, these biomes face severe threats from logging concessions (covering ~28% of the area), large-scale agriculture, and mining. This project aims to achieve systemic transformation by empowering customary landowners to adopt sustainable land use.

Project Components

A comprehensive approach to conserving critical forest biomes.

Component 1

Enabling Conditions

Developing supportive policies, including a potential national action plan on primary forest conservation.

Component 2

Area-Based Conservation

Protecting critical forest ecosystems and expanding the conservation estate.

Component 3

Integrated Management

Sustainable agri-food systems and forest management outside of protected areas.

Component 4

Innovative Finance

Creating sustainable investment mechanisms to support long-term conservation actions.

Component 5

Knowledge & Scaling

Ensuring access to technical expertise and sharing lessons learned.
